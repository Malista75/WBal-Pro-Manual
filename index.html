The Purpose of W'bal Pro

W'bal Pro transforms your Garmin watch into an advanced running power meter without needing an expensive footpod. By using data from your watch's GPS, barometer, and heart rate monitor, it provides real-time power metrics that are essential for serious training and racing.

The core concept is the W' Prime Balance (W'bal), which is visualized as a battery. This represents your anaerobic energy reserve—the energy you use for intense efforts like sprints or climbing steep hills. W'bal Pro helps you manage this energy perfectly, so you can push your limits without "bonking" before the finish line. It's designed for runners who want to train smarter, pace their races better, and understand their performance on a deeper level.

Understanding the Displayed Metrics

Total Power (W)

What it is:

This is the main, central number showing your current running power output in Watts. Unlike pace, which is affected by hills, or heart rate, which lags behind effort, power is an instantaneous and objective measure of how hard you are working.

Simplified Formula:

Power = Power to Run Flat + Power vs Wind + Power to Climb

W'bal (W' Balance)

What it is:

Represented by the battery icon, this is your finite reserve of anaerobic energy, measured in Joules. When you run harder than your sustainable "Critical Power" (CP), you drain this battery. When you run easier, it recharges. It's the ultimate tool for race pacing and interval training.

Simplified Formula:

If Power > CP, drain energy. If Power < CP, recharge energy.

GAP (Grade Adjusted Pace)

What it is:

This metric tells you what your pace would be if you were running on a completely flat surface. It allows you to maintain a consistent effort level when running on rolling hills, preventing you from overexerting on climbs or slacking off on descents.

Simplified Formula:

Adjusted Speed = Current Speed × Grade Factor

VO2 (Average VO2max)

What it is:

An ongoing estimate of your VO2max, a key indicator of your aerobic fitness. It represents the maximum amount of oxygen your body can use during intense exercise. Tracking this average can show fitness trends over time.

Simplified Formula:

VO2max ≈ (Power per kg) / (% of Max Heart Rate)

PF5 (Performance Factor)

What it is:

A comprehensive score of your running efficiency and performance for the current activity. It combines your power, speed, and cardiovascular response to give a single number. It is excellent for comparing different runs to see if your fitness is improving.

Simplified Formula:

Performance ≈ (Speed² × Power / Adjusted HR²) × Distance Factor

P3 / P10 / P20

What it is:

Your moving average power outputs over the last 3, 10, and 20 minutes. These are crucial for pacing. For example, in a 10k race, you might target a specific P10 value. They also help you set new personal records for power over those durations.

How it's calculated:

It uses an exponential moving average, which constantly updates and gives more weight to your most recent power data within that time window.

HR_Temp (Temperature-Adjusted HR)

What it is:

While not visible in this particular screenshot layout, some versions of W'bal Pro display HR_Temp. This is your Temperature-Adjusted Heart Rate. It estimates what your HR would be under neutral temperature conditions (e.g., 15°C / 59°F). This helps normalize metrics that use HR, like PF5, making them more comparable across runs in different weather.

Simplified Formula:

Adjusted HR = Current HR × Temperature Correction Factor

Behind the Scenes: Other Calculations

Slope (%)

Calculated from GPS and barometric altitude changes over distance, this is the current gradient of the terrain. It's a critical input for calculating both your Total Power and your Grade Adjusted Pace.

Relative Wind

Using the manual wind settings you provide, the data field calculates the wind you are actually experiencing based on your speed and direction of travel. A headwind increases this value, while a tailwind decreases it. This is used to determine the power needed to overcome air resistance.
